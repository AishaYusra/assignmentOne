My Mentor: MALALA YOUSAFZAI

Malala Yousafzai, the 26-year old Pakistani female education activist is someone i look up to because her story is indeed very inspiring, especially to the girl-child. She is the youngest Nobel Prize laureate, receiving the award only at the age of 17. She is a human rights advocate for the education of women and children in her homelad, Swat, where the Pakistani Taliban had at times banned girls from attending school. Her advocacy has in fact grown into an international movement. Several personalities including Barack Obama are her role models, but she was also inspired by her fathers humanitarian work who was also an activist. On the 9th of October, 2012, while on a bus in Swat District after taking an exam, Malala and two other girls were shot by a Taliban gunman in an assassination attampt, targeting Malala for her activism. The gunman fled the scene and unfortunatly she was struck in the head by a bullet. She was in critical condition at the Rawalpindi Institute of Crdiology after which she was transferred to the Queen Elizabeth Hospital in Birmingham, UK. The incident sparked an outpouring of support from all around the world and was even tagged as the most famous teenager in the world. Weeks after the attempted murder, a group of 50 leading Muslim clerics issued a fatwa against those who ried to kill her. The Pakistni Taliban were denounced by governments, human rights organizations and feminist groups. They, in retaliation also denounced Yousafzai, indicating plans to attempt another assassination on her life. After her recovery, Yousafzai became a prominent activist for the right to education. Based in Birmingham, she co-founded the MALALA FUND, a non-profit organization with Shiza Shahid. In 2013, she co-authored I AM MALALA, an international best seller. In 2012, she received Pakistani's first National Youth Peace Prize and the 2013 Sakharov Prize. In 2014, she was the co-recipient of the 2014 Nobel Peace Prize at the age of 17. In 2015, she was the subject of the Oscar-shortlisted documentary HE NAMED ME MALALA. The 2013,2014,2015 issues of TIME Magazine featured her as one of the most influential people gloally. In 2017, she was awarded honoay Canadian citizenship and became the youngest person to address the House of Commons of Canada. Yousafzai completed her secondary school education at Edgbaston High School, Birmingham in England. From there se won a place at Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford and studied Philosophy, Politics and Economics and graduated in 2020. She returned in 2023 to become the youngest ever Hpnorary fellow at Linacre College, Oxford. Yousafzai addressed the UN in July 2013, and had an audience with the Queen Elizabeth II in Buckingham Palace. In September, she spoke at Harvard University, and in October she met with US President Barack Obama and his family; during that meeting, she confronted him on his use of drone strikes in Pakistan. In December, she addressed the Oxford Union. In July 2014, Malala spoke at the Girl Summit in London. In October 2014, she donated $50,000 to the UNTWA for reconstruction of schools on the Gaza Strip. On 12 July 2015, her 18th Birthday, Malala opened a school in the Bekaa Valley, Lebanon, near the Syrian border, for Syrian refugees. These are just a few of the many achievements of Malala Yousafzai. She is currently happily married to Asser Malik.
