My Mentor

I have several mentors or role models as some would put it. But one that always stands out for me is the first wife of the prophet Muhammad SAW, Nana Khadijah bint Khuwaylid RA. She was born in c.555 or 567 in Makkah, Saudi Arabia. She had 6 children with the prophet SAW, 2 boys and 6 girls. In Islam, she is often referred to as The Mother of the Believers. She is also one of the four ladies of heaven. Khadijah RA, was a successful merchant. It is said that her caravans equaled that of the traders of Quraysh put together. She was given many honorifics, including Ameerat-Quraish; Princess of Quraish, The Pious One, and Khadijah al-Kubra;Khadijah the Great. She was a very wealthy woman and she used her wealth wisely by feeding and clothing the poor, assisting her relatives financially, and providing marriage portions for poor relations. In 595, Khadijah needed a co-worker for a transaction in Syria so she hired Muhammad SAW, then 25 years old for the trade in Syria sending word that she would pay to double her usual commission. Later on, following the Islamic way of marriage in Islam, she got married to the prophet Muhammad SAW. At that time he was 25 years old and she was 40 years old.When Muhammad SAW reported his first revelation from Angel Jibril, Khadijah RA was the first person to accept The Truth i.e. she accepted Islam. After his experience in the cave of Hira and Muhammad returned home i a state of terror, Khadijah comforted him with the words of Allah. Khadijah was supportive of Muhammad's prophetic mission, always helping in his work, proclaiming his message and belittling any opposition to his prophecies. Her constant encouragement helped Muhammad believe in his mission to spread Islam. When the polytheists and aristocrats of the Quraish harassed Muslims, she used her money to ransom muslim slaves and feed the muslim community. Khadijah died in Ramadan of the year 10 after the Prophethood. She is said to have been around 65 years old at the time of her death. She was buried in Janat al-Mu'alla cemetry, in Makkah, Saudi Arabia. Khadijah was a fine woman, she had several qualities that anyone would admire. She was highly regarded in society and in the eyes of Allah. I admire how she was very supportive to her relatives and the entire community at large and it never ran her pockets dry. This only proves that giving doesn't lessen your riches rather it enriches you even more. She was also a very loving and supportive wife which is a quality of hers that i admire a lot too. She was wealthy but she was still modest and humble in everything she approached.
She is one of my biggest inspirations because she lived a simple, yet fulfilled life.

Unfortunately, for obvious reasons, i have no image of her to insert. One thing is for sure though, she was a beautiful woman.
